The bank reconciliation document is a tool used to simplify a monthly bank closing process. It compares cash receipts, deposits, and invoice payments recorded in the system with the bank statement.

Bank reconciliations generate general journal transactions. For detailed information about transactions that are generated when bank reconciliation is posted, see the General journal help section.

To enter a bank reconciliation

  1. In the Bank section, click Bank reconciliations, and then click Create. 
  2. Enter or select the following:
    • Bank account. The bank account used in the bank statement.
    • Date. The bank reconciliation date.

      This field is prefilled with the current date. 
    • Statement from date and Statement to date. The start and end dates of the bank statement.

      The system will not allow you to enter a period that overlaps with another bank reconciliation.
       
      Once you fill the Statement to date field, the system fills the line items with documents that are reflected in the bank statement: Deposits, Invoice payments, and Cash purchases within the selected time period.
    • Beginning balance, Ending balance, Service charge, Service charge date, Interest earned, and Interest earned date. Fill these fields from your bank statement.
  3. Compare the amounts listed by the system with your bank statement and clear the amounts by selecting the Cleared check boxes against the cleared amounts.

    During the reconciliation process, the system updates the following fields in real time:
    • Cleared amount. The total amount for documents that are marked as cleared.
    • Cleared balance. Calculated as the sum of the cleared amount and the beginning balance.
    • Difference. The difference between the ending balance and the cleared balance.
    In case of a successful reconciliation the Difference is reduced to zero. Locate and fix discrepancies, if necessary.
  4. In the Memo field, you can enter notes on the bank reconciliation.
  5. Click Post and close.
