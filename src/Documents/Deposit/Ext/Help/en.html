The deposit is a document that records the following:

  • A bank deposit of one or several Cash receipts and Cash sales that were not deposited at the time of receiving.
  • A bank deposit from one or several G/L accounts to a bank G/L account.

Deposits generate general journal transactions. For detailed information about transactions that are generated when a deposit is posted, see the General journal help section.

To enter a deposit

  1. In the Bank section, click Deposits, and then click Create.
  2. Enter or select the following:
    • Number. By default the system assigns consecutive numbers when documents are saved, and no user input is required.

      You have the option to enter a custom number instead. Document numbers can include prefixes. For example, if you specify the number "D0001", your next deposits are automatically numbered "D0002", "D0003", and so on. 
    • Bank account. The account (e.g. bank, cash, credit card, PayPal, etc.) that will receive the funds. 
    • Date. The deposit date.

      This field is prefilled with the current date.
  3. In the Deposited documents list, select the check boxes next to the Cash receipts and Cash sales whose payments you want to deposit.

    The system displays all cash receipts and cash sales that have the Undeposited funds option selected and are not yet deposited.
  4. In the G/L accounts list, you can record a direct deposit to an account by entering accounts and amounts.

    You can use the "G/L accounts" section independently or combined with the "Deposited documents" section. 
  5. In the Memo field, you can enter notes on the deposit.
  6. Click Post and close.

    The informational (read-only) section at the bottom of the form contains the following data:
    • Total FCY. The total amount in the transaction currency.

      This field is only available if the Multicurrency check box is selected in Accounting settings. 
    • Total. The total amount in the reporting currency.
